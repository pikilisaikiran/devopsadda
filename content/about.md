## Hey there, future DevOps engineer 👋

**DevOps Adda** is a beginner-first learning hub built for people who want to break into DevOps without drowning in jargon. No prior ops experience required, just curiosity and a willingness to type commands into a terminal.

"Adda" means a gathering place, a spot where people hang out, swap ideas, and learn together. That's exactly the vibe here.

### What you'll find

- **Hands-on tutorials** that you can follow along with on your own machine
- A clear **[DevOps Roadmap](/roadmap/)** so you always know what to learn next
- Beginner guides to **Linux, Git, Docker, Kubernetes, CI/CD, Terraform**, and the cloud
- A curated **[Tools](/tools/)** page so you don't waste time figuring out what to install

### Who this is for

- Students and fresh grads eyeing a DevOps or Cloud role
- Developers who want to understand how their code ships to production
- Sysadmins moving into modern automation and infrastructure-as-code
- Anyone curious about how software actually gets built, tested, and deployed

### How to use this site

1. Start with the **[Roadmap](/roadmap/)** to see the big picture.
2. Work through the **[Tutorials](/posts/)** in order, or jump to a topic you care about.
3. Keep the **[Tools](/tools/)** page handy as your setup checklist.
4. Practice. Reading is good; breaking things in a terminal is better.
